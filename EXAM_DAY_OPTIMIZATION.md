# 🧠 EXAM DAY OPTIMIZATION GUIDE
**Science-Backed Nutrition, Sleep & Stress Management**

---

## 📅 TIMELINE: 3 DAYS BEFORE → EXAM DAY

### WEEK BEFORE EXAM (Last Prep Phase)

**Sleep Schedule (7-10 days before)**:
- [ ] Go to bed at same time EVERY night (builds sleep pressure)
- [ ] Aim 7-9 hours per night (no all-nighters!)
- [ ] Sleep deprivation → working memory crashes (GEB 331 = memory-heavy)
- [ ] **Best practice**: Stable sleep > 1 extra study hour

**Exercise Schedule**:
- [ ] Light exercise (30 min walking, yoga) = stress reduction ✓
- [ ] NO intense exercise 2 days before exam (waste of glycogen/energy)
- [ ] Stretching helps anxiety management

**Caffeine Baseline**:
- [ ] If you drink coffee daily, KEEP THE SAME AMOUNT
- [ ] Sudden caffeine increase → jitters, anxiety spikes
- [ ] Sudden withdrawal → headaches during exam
- [ ] **Standard dose**: If normal is 1 coffee/day, stick to 1

## ☀️ EXAM MORNING (Exam time + 3 hours)

### Wake-Up: 2-3 hours before exam

**Timing example: If exam is 10am, wake up at 7-7:30am**

**Immediate (First 15 min)**:
- [ ] No phone/social media (don't read anxiety-inducing news)
- [ ] Drink 250ml water (rehydrate overnight loss)
- [ ] Use bathroom (you'll go 2-3 times, so best to preempt)

**Breakfast (30-45 min before exam): 2:30 hours before start**

**OPTIMAL EXAM BREAKFAST** (based on glucose stability data):

**Option A - Complex Carb Focus**:
- 1 slice whole wheat toast
- 1 tbsp peanut butter or almond butter
- 1 banana
- 250ml orange juice (vitamin C + quick glucose)
- Black tea or 1 coffee (if normally drink coffee)

**Option B - Protein Focus** (if blood sugar gets jittery):
- 1-2 scrambled eggs
- 1 slice whole grain bread
- Handful of berries
- 200ml milk or yogurt
- Water or tea (no coffee if first time)

**Option C - Light & Quick** (if morning digestion issues):
- Oatmeal (half cup) with honey
- Banana
- 250ml orange juice
- Ginger tea (settles stomach)

**Why this combination?**
- Complex carbs (toast, banana) → sustained glucose release (2-3 hours)
- Protein (egg, peanut butter) → prevents mid-exam energy crash
- Glucose drink (OJ) → immediate energy boost (~10-15 min)
- Hydration → 5% dehydration = 10% cognitive drop

**Avoid**:
- ❌ Heavy breakfast (digestion takes blood from brain)
- ❌ High sugar (sugar crash at 1 hour into exam)
- ❌ New foods (digestive surprises!)
- ❌ Excess coffee (anxiety, jitters)

**Eat timing**: Finish breakfast 60-90 min BEFORE exam start
- Allows digestion to progress (peak awareness at exam time)
- Not so early that glucose drops again

## 📝 DURING EXAM (Hour 0-3)

### Timing Strategy (60 marks, 3 hours = 3 min per mark)

**First 5 minutes - READ ALL QUESTIONS** (critical!)
- ✓ Identify which Q are worth most marks (usually Q1, Q3, Q5, Q7 = 14m each)
- ✓ Identify which you feel confident about (mark with ✓)
- ✓ Identify which you're unsure about (mark with ?)

**Allocation strategy** (based on PATTERN_RECOGNITION):
- **Mechanism questions (40% of marks)**: Allocate 40% of time = ~72 min
- **Mutation consequence Q (25%)**: 25% time = ~45 min
- **Compare-contrast Q (15%)**: 15% time = ~27 min  
- **Clinical Q (10%)**: 10% time = ~18 min
- **Leave ~15 min for review**

**Specific timing**:
- Questions 1-3 (14m each): 14 min each = 42 min total
- Questions 4-5 (10m each): 9 min each = 18 min total
- Questions 6-8 (6-7m each): 6.5 min each = 19.5 min total
- Questions 9-10 (5m each): 5 min each = 10 min total
- **Review time**: 30 min to check answers + refine diagrams

---

### DURING EXAM - Hydration & Glucose Management

**Hour 1 (0-60 min)**:
- Sip 50ml water (not gulping - bathroom disruption)
- Glucose peak from breakfast still working ✓

**Hour 2 (60-120 min)**:
- [ ] Sip another 50ml water
- [ ] If allowed: Eat 1 glucose tablet or small banana piece (prevents crash)
- [ ] Glucose from breakfast declining → need boost for sustained focus
- [ ] This 5-sec break actually HELPS (attention refresh)

**Hour 3 (120-180 min)**:
- [ ] Final 50ml water
- [ ] Push through (final 30 min can feel slower, but you're almost done!)

### STRESS MANAGEMENT (if panic hits mid-exam)

**If you blank on an answer**:
1. **STOP writing** (don't panic-scribble)
2. **Take 3 deep breaths** (4-in, 6-out)
3. **Read the question again** (sometimes you misread stress)
4. **SKIP IT** - come back in review time (other questions rebuild confidence)
5. **Move to next question** (partial marks on others matter more than blanking multiple)

**If you think "I'm failing"**:
- Recognize: This is **catastrophic thinking** (anxiety response)
- Reality check: You studied! You know the material!
- Action: Write SOMETHING for every question (partial marks > blank)
- Remember: You only need 50% to pass (GEB 331 isn't harder than your previous exams)
