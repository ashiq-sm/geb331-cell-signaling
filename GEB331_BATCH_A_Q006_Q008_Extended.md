[Q006] Cell Signaling Molecules (2016, 14 marks)

🎯 KEYWORD BREAKDOWN:
• Hormone = "messenger chemical" — signals over long distances
• Ligand = "binding molecule" — the actual signal molecule
• Signaling molecule = "any chemical that carries information between cells"

📝 MNEMONIC:
🔡 "HGC-NLE" = Hormones, Growth factors, Cytokines - Neurotransmitters, Lipid mediators, Eicosanoids, gasses

✍️ COMPREHENSIVE SOLUTION:

a) **What are cell signaling molecules?** (5 marks)

Cell signaling molecules (সংকেত অণু) are chemical substances that carry information from one cell to another. These are diverse in structure but unified in function: communication & coordination.

**Major Classes of Signaling Molecules**:

1) **HORMONES** (হরমোন) — Proteins/Steroids/Peptides
   • Insulin (blood glucose regulation)
   • Cortisol (stress response)
   • Thyroid hormone (metabolism)
   • Secreted into bloodstream, travel systemically
   • Act on distant target cells
   • Long-lasting effects

2) **GROWTH FACTORS** (বৃদ্ধি কারক) — Small Proteins
   • EGF (Epidermal Growth Factor)
   • FGF (Fibroblast Growth Factor)
   • PDGF (Platelet-Derived GF)
   • NGF (Nerve Growth Factor)
   • Short-range (paracrine) signaling
   • Receptor tyrosine kinase activation

3) **CYTOKINES** (সাইটোকাইন) — Immune Signaling Proteins
   • Interleukins (IL-1, IL-2, IL-6, etc.)
   • TNF-α (Tumor Necrosis Factor)
   • Interferons (IFN-α, IFN-β, IFN-γ)
   • Mediate inflammation & immune response
   • Can act on multiple cell types

4) **NEUROTRANSMITTERS** (নিউরোট্রান্সমিটার) — Neural Signaling
   • Acetylcholine (ACh) — neuromuscular
   • Dopamine, serotonin — brain signals
   • GABA (γ-aminobutyric acid) — inhibitory
   • Glutamate, glycine — excitatory
   • Synaptic (ultra-precise, fast)

5) **LIPID MEDIATORS** — Membrane-Derived Signals
   • Prostaglandins (PG) — inflammatory
   • Leukotrienes (LT) — immune
   • Thromboxanes — platelet aggregation
   • Derived from arachidonic acid

6) **GASES** — Non-Traditional Signals
   • Nitric oxide (NO) — vessel dilation, neuronal signaling
   • Carbon monoxide (CO) — signaling molecule (emerging)
   • Hydrogen sulfide (H₂S) — vasorelaxant

📊 TABLE — Signaling Molecule Classification:

| Class | Size | Hydrophobicity | Receptor Location | Transport | Speed |
|-------|------|-----------------|-------------------|-----------|--------|
| **Hormone (protein)** | Large (50-200 AA) | Hydrophilic | Surface | Blood protein carriers | Slow (min-hrs) |
| **Growth Factor** | Small (20-30 kDa) | Hydrophilic | Surface | Direct diffusion | Medium (sec-min) |
| **Steroid Hormone** | Small (300 Da) | Lipophilic | Nuclear/Cytoplasm | Blood-binding proteins | Slow (hrs-days) |
| **Neurotransmitter** | Small (100-400 Da) | Mixed | Surface & cyto | Vesicular release | Ultra-fast (ms) |
| **Prostaglandin** | Small (350 Da) | Lipophilic | Surface & nuclear | Direct diffusion | Fast (sec) |
| **Gas (NO)** | Tiny (30 Da) | Lipophilic | Soluble (cytoplasm) | Diffusion | Ultra-fast (ms) |

b) **Different Types of Signal Methods** (5 marks)

Signal methods (পাঠানোর পদ্ধতি) = distinct communication geometries:

1. **ENDOCRINE** (হরমোনাল):
   • Long-range (systemic via bloodstream)
   • Slow onset (minutes to hours)
   • Broad distribution (everywhere with receptors)
   • Sustained effects
   • Example: Insulin→pancreas→travels blood→liver/muscle

2. **PARACRINE** (স্থানীয়):
   • Short-range (adjacent cells, micrometers)
   • Medium speed (seconds to minutes)
   • Local neighborhood signaling
   • Growth factors between epithelial & stromal cells
   • Example: FGF from fibroblasts→epithelial cells

3. **AUTOCRINE** (স্ব-সংকেত):
   • Self-signaling (cell responds to its own signal)
   • Amplification loop (positive feedback)
   • Or self-limitation (negative feedback)
   • Example: T-cell producing IL-2, then responding to it

4. **SYNAPTIC** (স্নায়বিক):
   • Ultra-precise (neuron→specific target)
   • Fastest (milliseconds)
   • Neurotransmitter across synaptic cleft
   • Highly localized
   • Example: ACh at neuromuscular junction

5. **JUXTACRINE** (যোগাযোগ-নির্ভর):
   • Contact-dependent (must be physically adjacent)
   • Membrane-bound ligands
   • Cell-cell recognition signals
   • Example: Notch-Delta signaling in cell fate decisions

c) **Examples: Hydrophilic vs Hydrophobic Signaling Molecules** (4 marks)

| Property | HYDROPHILIC (জল-প্রিয়) | HYDROPHOBIC (জল-বিদ্বেষী) |
|----------|------------|------------|
| **Solubility** | Dissolves in water | Insoluble in water |
| **Transport** | Bloodstream freely, or protein-bound | Only travel protein-bound |
| **Receptor** | Surface (membrane) | Intracellular or nuclear |
| **Speed** | Faster (direct diffusion) | Slower (need transport proteins) |
| **Examples** | Insulin, epinephrine, IL-6 | Cortisol, estrogen, thyroid hormone |
| **Action** | Exocytosis release | Diffusion through membranes |

**Hydrophilic Examples & Key Points**:
• **Insulin** — 51 amino acids, secreted by pancreatic β-cells, acts on liver/muscle/fat (GLUT4 insertion via PKB signaling)
• **Epinephrine** — small catecholamine, released from adrenal medulla, acts on β-AR (GPCR)
• **Glucagon** — 29 amino acids, pancreatic α-cells, raises blood glucose (activates glycogenolysis via PKA)
• **IL-6** — cytokine, 184 AA, produced by macrophages, triggers acute phase response

**Hydrophobic Examples & Key Points**:
• **Cortisol** — steroid hormone, acts via glucocorticoid receptor (GR) → nuclear translocation → gene expression
• **Aldosterone** — mineralocorticoid, regulates Na⁺/K⁺ balance via nuclear receptor
• **Estrogen** — steroid, acts via estrogen receptor (ER), regulates reproductive & metabolic genes
• **Thyroid hormone** (T3/T4) — iodinated tyrosine derivatives, acts via thyroid hormone receptor (TR), affects metabolism globally

💡 Key Principle: **Hydrophobic nature determines LOCATION of receptor & SPEED of response**
• Hydrophilic → surface receptor → fast (no membrane barrier)
• Hydrophobic → nuclear receptor → slower (must cross membranes, enter nucleus)

[Q007] Orphan and Nuclear Receptors (2016, 14 marks)

🎯 KEYWORD BREAKDOWN:
• Orphan = "parentless" — no known natural ligand initially
• Nuclear receptor = "ligand-activated transcription factor" (both receiver & effector)
• Response element = DNA sequence where TF binds

📝 MNEMONIC:
🔡 "ODE-NR" = Orphan Deorphanized Ever - Nuclear Receptors
  Narrative: Orphan receptors are like "lost children" — eventually we find their parents (ligands)!
  
✍️ COMPREHENSIVE SOLUTION:

a) **What are Orphan Receptors?** (4 marks)

Orphan receptors (অনাথ গ্রাহক) are nuclear receptors whose natural/endogenous ligands were initially UNKNOWN.

**Historical Context**:
• Discovered via molecular cloning, sequence simiarity to known nuclear receptors
• Named before identifying ligand function
• Some still remain "orphaned" (no ligand found)
• Process of identifying ligands = "deorphanization" (সন্তান খুঁজে পাওয়া)

**Examples of Deorphanization Success**:
1. LXR (Liver X Receptor) — orphan → FOUND LIGAND: oxysterols (oxidized cholesterol)
2. FXR (Farnesoid X Receptor) — orphan → FOUND LIGAND: bile acids
3. PXR (Pregnane X Receptor) — orphan → FOUND LIGAND: xenobiotics & drugs
4. CAR (Constitutive Androstane Receptor) — orphan → FOUND LIGAND: androstenol & drugs

**True Orphans** (still no confirmed endogenous ligand):
• Some NRs assumed to have ligands that are:
  - Tissue-specific & rare
  - Environmental factors
  - Metabolites awaiting discovery

💡 Biological Significance: Orphan receptors likely regulate important processes but signaling pathways not yet elucidated.

b) **"Nuclear Receptors are Receptors AND Effectors for Hydrophobic Signals"** — Explain (5 marks)

This statement captures the DUAL FUNCTION of nuclear receptors:

**What Makes Nuclear Receptors Unique**:

Traditional signaling paradigm:
```
Ligand → Receptor → Signal Transduction → Transcription Factor → Gene Response
(extracellular) (membrane) (intracellular cascade) (downstream) (nucleus)
```

Nuclear receptor paradigm (ONE protein does TWO jobs):
```
Ligand → NUCLEAR RECEPTOR (acts as BOTH receiver AND effector) → Gene Response
(enters nucleus directly)     (receives signal + causes response)
```

**Structural Features Enabling Dual Function**:

1. **Ligand-Binding Domain (LBD)** — RECEPTOR function:
   • High-affinity binding pocket for hydrophobic ligands
   • Distinguishes between different steroid hormones (estrogen vs androgen)
   • Undergoes conformational change upon ligand binding
   • Example: Estrogen receptor (ER) has deep pocket that fits estrogen (270 Da)

2. **DNA-Binding Domain (DBD)** — EFFECTOR function:
   • Zinc finger motif (two zinc atoms coordinated by cysteines)
   • Recognizes specific DNA sequences (hormone response elements — HREs)
   • Binds promoter regions of target genes
   • Half-site sequences (e.g., GRE = GlucocorticoidResponseElement: AGACA)

3. **Activation Function Domains (AF1/AF2)** — transactivation:
   • AF1 = ligand-independent activity (constitutive)
   • AF2 = ligand-dependent activation (inducible)
   • Recruit coactivator proteins (CBP, SRC-1)

**Mechanism of Nuclear Receptor Action**:

Step-by-step pathway:
```
1. Hydrophobic hormone (cortisol, estrogen) enters cytoplasm
   ↓
2. Binds nuclear receptor → conformational change
   ↓
3. Receptor-ligand complex translocates to nucleus
   ↓
4. Complex binds HRE (hormone response element) in promoter
   ↓
5. Recruits coactivators & chromatin remodeling complex
   ↓
6. Increases accessibility of promoter to RNA polymerase II
   ↓
7. Target gene transcription ↑↑↑
   ↓
8. mRNA → new proteins → CELLULAR RESPONSE
```

**Why This is "Receptor AND Effector"**:
• Most signaling proteins: receptor recognizes signal; separate effector causes response
• Nuclear receptor: SINGLE protein acts as BOTH signal detector (LBD) AND response effector (DBD+transactivation)
• Direct coupling of signal sensing to gene regulation (most efficient!)

🔀 TABLE — Nuclear Receptor vs Traditional Signaling:

| Feature | Traditional GPCR | Nuclear Receptor |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Ligand Type** | Hydrophilic | Hydrophobic |
| **Receptor Location** | Plasma membrane | Cytoplasm/nucleus |
| **Mechanism** | Activates intermediate proteins | Direct DNA binding |
| **Gene Regulation** | Via TF phosphorylation (multi-step) | Direct (single protein) |
| **Speed** | Fast (seconds) | Slow (minutes-hours) |
| **Efficiency** | Amplification-based | Direct (high signal-to-response ratio) |
| **Example** | Epinephrine→β-AR→GPCR→PKA→TF phospho | Cortisol→GR→nucleus→gene ON |

c) **Classification & Functions of Nuclear Receptors** (5 marks)

**Major Families of Nuclear Receptors** (핵수용체 분류):

1. **STEROID RECEPTORS** (스테로이드 수용체):
   • Glucocorticoid Receptor (GR) — cortisol → anti-inflammation, stress response
   • Mineralocorticoid Receptor (MR) — aldosterone → Na⁺/K⁺ balance
   • Estrogen Receptor (ER) — estrogen → reproductive development, neuroprotection
   • Androgen Receptor (AR) — testosterone → male characteristics, muscle growth
   • Progesterone Receptor (PR) — progesterone → reproduction

2. **THYROID HORMONE RECEPTORS** (갑상선호르몬 수용체):
   • TR-α, TR-β — T3/T4 → metabolism, growth, development, thermogenesis

3. **RETINOID RECEPTORS** (레티노이드 수용체):
   • Retinoic Acid Receptor (RAR) — retinoic acid (Vit A metabolite) → development, differentiation
   • Retinoid X Receptor (RXR) — heterodimerizes with many other NRs

4. **LIPID-SENSING RECEPTORS** (지질감지 수용체):
   • LXR (Liver X Receptor) — oxysterols → cholesterol metabolism
   • FXR (Farnesoid X Receptor) — bile acids → bile acid & glucose metabolism
   • PPARs (Peroxisome Proliferator-Activated Receptors) — fatty acids → lipid metabolism

5. **ORPHAN/ADOPTED RECEPTORS** (미확인 수용체) — increasingly being liganded

**General Functions of Nuclear Receptors** (핵수용체 기능):

| Function | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **Metabolism** | Glucocorticoid ↑ gluconeogenesis; PPAR ↑ fatty acid oxidation |
| **Development** | Retinoic acid → embryonic patterning; sex hormones → sexual differentiation |
| **Differentiation** | PPAR-γ → adipogenesis (differentiate to fat cells) |
| **Stress Adaptation** | GR → cortisol response to stress (immune suppression, glucose mobilization) |
| **Reproduction** | ER, AR, PR → sexual development, fertility, pregnancy |
| **Anti-Inflammation** | GR → suppress cytokine production (IL-6, TNF-α) |
| **Thermogenesis** | TR → increase metabolic rate, heat production (brown fat) |
| **Detoxification** | CAR, PXR → xenobiotic metabolism (drug-metabolizing enzymes) |

**Hormone Response Elements (HREs)** — DNA sequences:
• Palindromic sequences (read same forward/backward)
• Nuclear receptors often bind as HOMODIMERS or HETERODIMERS on HRE
• Example: GRE = "AGAACA...TGTTCT" (half-sites spaced 3 bp apart)
• Different spacing & orientation = tissue-specific & gene-specific regulation

📚 References:
• [Alberts Ch.17, "Nuclear Receptors & Gene Regulation"]
• [Lehninger Ch.12, "Nuclear Receptors & Hormone Action"]

[Q008] Cell-to-Cell Communication (2014, 14 marks)

🎯 KEYWORD BREAKDOWN:
• Communication = "sharing of information between entities"
• Coordination = "synchronized activity toward common goal"
• Entity = "organized structure with defined properties"

📝 MNEMONIC:
🔡 "CELL-COM" = Coordination, External ligands, Local/Long-range, Ligand-receptor, Communication, Organization, Messaging

✍️ COMPREHENSIVE SOLUTION:

a) **What is cell-to-cell communication? Basic tools involved.** (5 marks)

**Definition**:
Cell-to-cell communication (কোষ-থেকে-কোষ যোগাযোগ) = exchange of regulatory information among cells to coordinate tissue and organism-level functions.

**Why Communication is Essential** (কেন যোগাযোগ প্রয়োজন):
• Organisms composed of >37 trillion cells
• Need synchronized behavior (heartbeat, breathing, metabolism)
• Growth/development requires patterning signals
• Immune response requires cell signaling (lymphocytes calling other cells)
• Tissue homeostasis depends on nutrient sensing & feedback

**Basic Tools of Cell-to-Cell Communication** (যোগাযোগের হাতিয়ার):

1. **SIGNALING MOLECULES** (রাসায়নিক সংকেত):
   • Hormones, growth factors, neurotransmitters, cytokines
   • Traveling via blood, diffusion, or vesicular release
   • Carry specific "message" (identity/concentration)

2. **RECEPTORS** (গ্রাহক):
   • Specific macromolecules recognizing signal molecules
   • Lock-key specificity (hormone + receptor = unique pair)
   • Located on cell surface (hydrophilic signals) or inside (hydrophobic)

3. **TRANSDUCTION MACHINERY** (পরিবহন যন্ত্র):
   • Relay proteins converting extracellular signal → intracellular action
   • G-proteins, kinases, phosphatases, lipases
   • Create signal amplification cascade

4. **SECOND MESSENGERS** (দ্বিতীয় বার্তা):
   • Small diffusible intermediates (cAMP, IP₃, DAG, Ca²⁺)
   • Spread signal throughout cytoplasm
   • Amplify signal (one receptor → many messengers)

5. **EFFECTOR PROTEINS** (প্রভাবক):
   • Execute cellular response
   • Kinases (add phosphate), phosphatases (remove), enzymes
   • Channel proteins (regulate ion flow)
   • Transcription factors (alter gene expression)

6. **GAP JUNCTIONS** (ফাঁক সংযোগ):
   • Channels between adjacent cells (especially connected tissues)
   • Allow direct cytoplasmic communication
   • Connexon proteins forming hemichannels
   • Permit passage of ions & small molecules
   • Coordin ate cardiac muscle contraction, smooth muscle waves, neuronal networks

7. **CELL-CELL ADHESION MOLECULES** (আঠা প্রোটিন):
   • Cadherins (calcium-dependent adhesins)
   • Integrins (extracellular matrix receptors)
   • Immunoglobulins (NCAM, ICAM, VCAM)
   • Provide physical contact + generate signaling

8. **EXTRACELLULAR MATRIX** (বহিঃকোশীয় জাল):
   • Protein scaffolding (collagen, fibronectin, laminin)
   • Stores growth factors & cytokines
   • Integrin binding → signal transduction
   • Provides mechanical stability & communication substrate

**Summary Table — Communication Tools**:

| Tool | Function | Location | Speed |
|------|----------|----------|-------|
| Hormone/Growth factor | Signal molecule | Secreted/Blood | Minutes-hours |
| Receptor | Detection | Cell surface/Nucleus | Milliseconds |
| Transducer (G-protein, kinase) | Signal relay | Cytoplasm | Milliseconds-seconds |
| Second messenger | Amplification & spreading | Cytoplasm | Milliseconds |
| Effector | Response execution | Cytoplasm/Nucleus | Seconds-minutes |
| Gap junction | Direct communication | Cell-cell interface | Milliseconds |

b) **Intracellular & Extracellular Signaling Pathways; Classification** (5 marks)

**EXTRACELLULAR Signaling** (বহিঃকোশীয় সংকেত):
= Communication from one cell to another, OUTSIDE the cell
= Ligand delivery from signaling cell → target cell

**Types of Extracellular Signaling Pathways**:

1. **ENDOCRINE** (হরমোনাল):
   • Signal via bloodstream (hormone travels systemically)
   • Long distance, long duration
   • Example: Pancreatic β-cells secrete insulin → reaches liver 10 cm away

2. **NEUROENDOCRINE** (স্নায়ু-হরমোনাল):
   • Neurons secrete hormones into bloodstream (hypothalamic-pituitary axis)
   • Examples: ADH, oxytocin from hypothalamus

3. **PARACRINE** (স্থানীয়):
   • Signal diffuses locally to nearby cells (<100 μm away)
   • Growth factors, cytokines
   • Example: Fibroblast FGF→epithelial cell

4. **SYNAPTIC** (আশ্রয়স্থল):
   • Neurotransmitter across synaptic cleft (20-40 nm gap)
   • Ultra-fast, highly precise
   • Example: Motor neuron→muscle via acetylcholine

5. **JUXTACRINE** (যোগাযোগ):
   • Physical contact required (no diffusion)
   • Membrane-bound ligands
   • Example: Notch-Delta, immune cell interactions

**INTRACELLULAR Signaling** (অন্তঃকোশীয় সংকেত):
= Signal transduction events INSIDE the target cell
= After ligand reception, how signal is processed intracellularly

**Cascade Events** (ধারাবাহিক ঘটনা):
```
Ligand Binding
    ↓
Receptor conformational change
    ↓
G-protein activation (or kinase autophosphorylation)
    ↓
Second messenger formation
    ↓
Effector protein activation
    ↓
Phosphorylation cascade
    ↓
Transcription factor activation
    ↓
Gene expression change
    ↓
CELLULAR RESPONSE
```

**Classification of Intracellular Signaling Pathways** (অন্তঃকোশীয় পথ শ্রেণীবিভাগ):

1. **BY RECEPTOR TYPE**:
   • GPCR pathway (G-proteins, second messengers, kinase cascades)
   • RTK pathway (autophosphorylation, SH2 domains, MAPK cascades)
   • TF/nuclear receptor pathway (direct DNA binding)
   • Ion-channel linked pathway (direct ion flow altering membrane potential)

2. **BY SIGNALING MODE**:
   • Transient signaling (rapid activation → rapid termination) — synaptic
   • Sustained signaling (prolonged effect) — endocrine
   • Oscillatory signaling (pulses) — Ca²⁺/MAPK oscillations

3. **BY ENDPOINT**:
   • Metabolic response (immediate enzyme activation)
   • Gene expression response (hours → new mRNA/protein)
   • Developmental response (days-weeks → cell fate change)
   • Movement response (second → immediate behavior)

**Integrated Communication Model** (সমন্বিত যোগাযোগ মডেল):
```
EXTRACELLULAR (বহিঃকোশীয়)          INTRACELLULAR (অন্তঃকোশীয়)
Signaling Cell    Target Cell         Target Cell Nucleus
    ↓                 ↓                      ↓
Hormone/GF        Receptor            Transcription Factor
  released        recognition         activation
    │                 │                     │
    └─→ Diffusion/Blood ───→ Binds ────→ Cascade ───→ Gene ON/OFF
        (minutes)          (ms)       (seconds)     (hours)
```

c) **Define Cellular Receptors; Characteristics** (4 marks)

**Definition**:
Cellular receptor (কোষীয় গ্রাহক) = macromolecule that specifically recognizes and binds signaling molecules (ligands), undergoes conformational change, and initiates cellular response.

**Characteristics of Cellular Receptors** (কোষীয় গ্রাহক বৈশিষ্ট্য):

1. **SPECIFICITY** (নির্দিষ্টতা):
   • Each receptor binds specific ligand(s) with high affinity
   • Different hormone → different receptor
   • Ensures correct message reaches intended target
   • Rare: promiscuous receptors binding multiple ligands

2. **SATURABILITY** (সীমাবদ্ধতা):
   • Finite number of receptors per cell (1,000–1,000,000 depending on receptor)
   • At high ligand concentration, all receptors occupied
   • Follows Michaelis-Menten kinetics (Kd = binding affinity)
   • Allows cells to regulate response magnitude

3. **REVERSIBILITY** (প্রত্যাবর্তনশীলতা):
   • Ligand-receptor binding is reversible
   • Ligand + Receptor ⇌ Ligand-Receptor complex
   • Allows rapid on/off signaling
   • Dissociation rate (Koff) determines signal duration

4. **REGULATED EXPRESSION** (নিয়ন্ত্রিত প্রকাশ):
   • Receptor protein levels vary by:
     - Cell type (muscle lacks pancreatic hormone receptors)
     - Developmental stage (fetal development ≠ adult receptor profile)
     - Physiological state (stress increases GR expression)
     - Feedback (high hormone concentration ↓ receptor expression — "downregulation")
   • Allows cells to CUSTOMIZE signaling sensitivity

5. **DESENSITIZATION** (সংবেদনশীলতা হ্রাস):
   • Prolonged ligand exposure → decreased cellular response
   • Mechanisms:
     - Receptor phosphorylation (β-adrenergic receptor kinase)
     - Receptor internalization (endocytosis)
     - Receptor degradation (ubiquitin-proteasomal pathway)
   • Protects cells from overstimulation
   • Allows body to adapt to sustained hormones

6. **AMPLIFICATION POTENTIAL** (বর্ধন সামর্থ্য):
   • One receptor activation → many intracellular signals
   • Cascade multiplication: 1 GPCR → N G-proteins → M adenylyl cyclases → P cAMP molecules
   • Result: strong response from weak signal
   • Critical for detecting low-concentration hormones

7. **TISSUE & CELLULAR SPECIFICITY** (টিস্যু-নির্দিষ্ট):
   • Different tissues express different receptors
   • Epinephrine effects on heart ≠ epinephrine effects on liver (different receptors/downstream proteins)
   • Same hormone, different outcome per tissue
   • Enables coordinated but contextualized responses

8. **COUPLING EFFICIENCY** (সংযোগ দক্ষতা):
   • Varies from tight coupling (immediate response) to loose coupling (delayed)
   • Depends on G-protein expression, effector proximity, second messenger levels
   • G-protein coupled receptors: often 1 receptor can activate multiple G-proteins
   • RTKs: one receptor can recruit multiple downstream targets

**Summary Table — Receptor Characteristics**:

| Characteristic | Meaning | Physiological Significance |
|---|---|---|
| Specificity | Unique ligand recognition | Enables targeted signaling (hormone doesn't activate all paths) |
| Saturability | Limited receptor number | Cells can control response magnitude |
| Reversibility | On/off switching | Rapid signal termination possible |
| Regulated expression | Receptor levels change | Adaptation to chronic stimuli |
| Desensitization | Prolonged exposure → ↓ response | Protects from overstimulation |
| Amplification | Cascade multiplication | Detects low-concentration signals |
| Specificity | Tissue/cell type variation | Same signal, different outcomes per context |

• [Lehninger Ch.12, "Receptor Classification & Properties"]
