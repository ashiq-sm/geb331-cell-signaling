Q026: Ras-MAPK Feedback (2015, 8 marks)
├─ Mnemonic: "RAS-FEEDBACK-LOOP" = Ras-mediated | Amplified | Suppressed via proteolysis
├─ Topic: MAPK cascade negative feedback
├─ Solution: Ras recruits RAF → RAF dimerization → MEK activation → ERK phosphorylation
│           ERK phosphorylates Sprouty (inhibitor) + DUSP (phosphatase)
│           Sprouty blocks SOS recruitment; DUSP removes ERK phosphates
│           Self-limiting design prevents runaway proliferation
├─ Table: Feedback layer | Mechanism | Timescale | Strength
├─ Citation: [Alberts Ch.16, Negative Feedback]; [Lehninger Ch.12]
└─ Exam insight: Explain why GPCR responses are temporary while growth factors persistent

Q027: Notch Pathway - Ligand & Receptor (2013, 12 marks)
├─ Mnemonic: "NOTCH-CONTACTS" = Notch oligomerization | Trans-membrane | CHOP/CSL
├─ Topic: Developmental signaling - Notch canonical pathway
├─ Solution: Delta/Jagged ligand binds Notch extracellular domain
│           Ligand-induced endocytosis of Notch ectodomain by ligand-expressing cell
│           ADAM cleaves Notch near membrane → NICD (Notch intracellular domain) released
│           NICD enters nucleus → binds CSL (CBF1/RBPjK) → recruits Mastermind
│           Mastermind bridges NICD-CSL interaction
│           Complex activates Notch target genes (HES1, HERP - lateral inhibition)
│           Lateral inhibition: Delta+ cell → Notch signal in neighbors → ↓ Delta in neighbors
│           Result: Salt-and-pepper pattern (mosaic of Delta+ and Delta- cells)
├─ Table: Component | Structure | Function | Disease if mutated
├─ Clinical: NOTCH1 mutations → T-cell lymphoma; NOTCH3 → CADASIL (stroke)
└─ Exam: Compare Notch vs Wnt vs RTK signaling pathways

Q028: Wnt Signaling & β-Catenin (2016, 10 marks)
├─ Mnemonic: "WNT-BETA-GENES" = Wnt ligand | β-catenin Stability | Target gene activation
├─ Solution: Wnt secreted protein binds Frizzled (7-TM) + LDL receptor-related protein (LRP)
│           Normal (no Wnt): β-catenin phosphorylated by GSK3β (priming)
│           CK1α phosphorylates primed β-catenin → β-TrCP ubiquitin ligase recognizes
│           β-catenin degraded by proteasome
│           With Wnt: DVL (Dishevelled) recruits GSK3β away from β-catenin
│           β-catenin accumulates → enters nucleus → binds TCF/LEF transcription factors
│           LEF-β-catenin drives target genes: cyclin D1, c-myc, Axin2
│           Canonical vs non-canonical: Canonical = β-catenin/TCF; non-canonical = PCP, Ca²⁺
├─ Table: Condition | β-catenin level | TCF/LEF | Outcome
├─ Diseases: APC mutations (colorectal cancer) → uncontrolled Wnt (β-catenin always stable)
└─ Exam: Continuous Wnt (APC loss) → cancer explanation

Q029: NF-κB Activation by TNFα (2017, 14 marks)
├─ Mnemonic: "TNF-IKK-NF" = Tumor Necrosis Factor | IκB Kinase | Nuclear Factor-κB
├─ Solution: TNFα binds TNFR1 (death domain receptor)
│           TNFR1 recruits TRADD (TNF receptor-associated death domain protein)
│           TRADD recruits RIP kinase + TRAF2 (TNF receptor-associated factor)
│           TRAF2 recruits IKK (inhibitor κB kinase) complex = IKKα, IKKβ, NEMO
│           IKK performs trans-autophosphorylation → becomes active
│           Active IKK phosphorylates IκB (inhibitor of κB protein) at Ser32/36
│           Phosphorylated IκB recognized by β-TrCP → ubiquitinated → degraded
│           IκB removal exposes NLS (nuclear localization signal) on NF-κB
│           NF-κB (p65/p50 heterodimer) enters nucleus
│           NF-κB binds κB sites in promoters (GGGACTTTCC palindrome sequence)
│           NF-κB recruits CBP (coactivator) → gene transcription (IL-8, TNFα, Bcl-2)
│           Signal termination: NF-κB target genes include IκB + A20 (negative feedback)
│           New IκB made → re-binds NF-κB → removal from nucleus → recycling
├─ Table: Component | Phosphorylation | Effect | Disease if mutated
├─ Clinical: Over-active NF-κB → inflammatory diseases (RA, IBD); survival in lymphomas
└─ Exam: Contrast NF-κB acute (TNFα) vs chronic activation mechanisms

Q030: TGF-β Smad Pathway (2014, 12 marks)
├─ Mnemonic: "TGF-SMAD-GENES" = TGF-β ligand | Smad relay | Activates target genes
├─ Solution: TGF-β dimer binds ALK5 (activin receptor-like kinase 5) + TGFR2
│           ALK5 (Ser/Thr kinase) phosphorylates R-Smad (receptor-activated Smad)
│           R-Smad2/3 phosphorylated at C-terminal (pSmad2/3)
│           pSmad2/3 binds Co-Smad (common Smad) = Smad4
│           pSmad2/3-Smad4 complex enters nucleus
│           Nucleus complex binds Smad binding elements (SBE) in promoters
│           Recruits coactivators or corepressors (context-dependent)
│           TGF-β target genes: p21 (CDK inhibitor - halt cell cycle), PAI-1 (plasminogen activator inhibitor)
│           TGF-β also drives EMT (epithelial-mesenchymal transition) genes: Snail, Slug
│           EMT requires: ↓ E-cadherin (epithelial) + ↑ N-cadherin, vimentin (mesenchymal)
│           Crosstalk: MAPK can phosphorylate Smad → modified response
├─ Table: Smad type | Activation | Localization | Function
├─ Clinical: TGF-β hyper-activation → fibrosis (excessive collagen), invasive cancer
└─ Exam: TGF-β dual role: tumor suppressor (early) vs pro-metastatic (late)
