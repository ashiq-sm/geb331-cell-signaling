## 🔑 CRITICAL CONCEPTS MASTERED

### ⭐ **MUST-KNOW for Exams**

1. **Ala-146 Mechanism (Q014)** — The "dirty hands" concept from G1-2025.pdf
   - How alanine-146 discriminates GTP vs GDP through contact with γ-phosphate
   - Why Ala-146 is oncologically significant (mutations in Ras cause cancer)
   - RGS proteins accelerate GTPase 50-1000 fold

2. **Signal Amplification (Q015)** — 10,000,000× total amplification
   - 1 → 10 (GPCR multiplicity) → 100 (adenylyl cyclase) → 1000 (cAMP synthesis) → 10,000 (PKA) → 1,000,000 (phosphorylations)
   - Why nanomolar hormones cause cellular responses

3. **Gβγ Independent Signaling (Q016)** — NOT just a Gα inhibitor
   - Gβγ activates PLC-β, ion channels, PI3K independently
   - Cardiac M2 muscarinic: Gα↓cAMP + Gβγ↑K⁺ = synergistic bradycardia

4. **Phototransduction (Q022)** — Millisecond temporal resolution
   - cGMP cascade (not cAMP) for speed
   - RGS9 accelerates transducin GTPase
   - Why vision requires nanosecond-unit timing

5. **Cholera Toxin (Q022)** — ADP-ribosylation mechanism
   - Permanently activates Gαs (breaks GTPase)
   - Causes massive cAMP → ion secretion → diarrhea
   - Clinical: 50% mortality without rehydration

6. **Rb-E2F G1-S Transition (Q025)** — Cell cycle gating
   - Hypophosphorylated Rb sequesters E2F
   - Cyclin D/CDK4 + E/CDK2 phosphorylate Rb
   - E2F drives S-phase genes (cyclin A, DNA polymerase)

7. **RTK Autophosphorylation (Q023)** — Initiates cascade
   - Ligand-induced dimerization
   - Trans-autophosphorylation activates kinase domain
   - Grb2-SH2 recognizes phosphotyrosine docking sites
