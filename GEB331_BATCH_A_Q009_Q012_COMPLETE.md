[Q009] Cell Signaling Types (2017, 7 marks)

🎯 KEYWORD BREAKDOWN:
• "Intracellular signal molecules" = দ্বিতীয় দূত (second messengers) — cAMP, IP₃, Ca²⁺, DAG

📝 MNEMONIC:
🔡 "CAMP-ICC" = cAMP, MAPK, Phosphorylation - Ion flux, Cytoskeletal changes, Chromatin remodeling

✍️ COMPREHENSIVE SOLUTION:

a) **Cell-to-Cell Communication + Functions of Intracellular Signal Molecules** (1+4 marks)

Cell-to-cell communication (কোষ-থেকে-কোষ যোগাযোগ) = coordinated information exchange enabling tissue/organism function (defined in Q008).

Functions of Intracellular Signal Molecules (অন্তঃকোশীয় সংকেত অণুর কাজ):

1. **AMPLIFICATION** (বর্ধন):
   • One activated receptor generates MANY second messengers
   • Example: One GPCR activation → N G-proteins → M adenylyl cyclases → P cAMP molecules
   • Result: exponential signal multiplication (1→10→100→1000)
   • Allows cell to detect nanomolar ligand concentrations

2. **SPATIAL PROPAGATION** (স্থান সম্প্রসারণ):
   • Small diffusible molecules spread throughout cytoplasm
   • Create intracellular calcium waves (Ca²⁺ oscillations)
   • cAMP/IP₃ gradients control cell polarity & movement
   • Enable coordinated responses in large cells

3. **TEMPORAL REGULATION** (সময় নিয়ন্ত্রণ):
   • Short half-lives (seconds for cAMP; milliseconds for Ca²⁺ oscillations)
   • Rapid ON-OFF switching (unlike slow gene expression)
   • Frequency encoding (Ca²⁺ pulse frequency → different cellular decisions)

4. **INTEGRATION OF SIGNALS** (সংকেত সমন্বয়):
   • Multiple pathways converge on same effectors
   • Example: Both GPCR-PKA AND RTK-ERK phosphorylate shared substrates
   • Allows "voting" on cellular response

5. **FEEDBACK REGULATION** (প্রতিক্রিয়া নিয়ন্ত্রণ):
   • Positive feedback: cAMP activates PKA → phosphorylates membrane receptors → sensitizes response
   • Negative feedback: High Ca²⁺ inhibits IP₃ receptor → stops further Ca²⁺ release
   • Prevents over-signaling & enables adaptation

b) **Effector Protein & Second Messenger; Properties of Second Messengers** (1+1+3 marks)

**Effector Protein** (প্রভাবক প্রোটিন) Definition:
= Protein that PRODUCES the cellular response (not just relay)
= Enzymes or channels executing downstream biochemical work

Examples:
• **PKA** (protein kinase A) — phosphorylates target proteins
• **ERK** (extracellular signal-regulated kinase) — phosphorylates transcription factors
• **PLC** (phospholipase C) — cleaves PIP₂ into IP₃ + DAG
• **Adenylyl cyclase** — synthesizes cAMP from ATP

**Second Messenger** (দ্বিতীয় দূত) Definition:
= Small, diffusible mediator molecule that relays signal from receptor to effector

Examples: cAMP, IP₃, DAG, Ca²⁺, ROS (reactive oxygen species)

**COMPARISON: Effector vs Second Messenger**

| Feature | Effector Protein | Second Messenger |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Nature** | Protein (large) | Small molecule (100-400 Da) |
| **Synthesis** | Gene expression (slow) | Enzymatic synthesis (fast) |
| **Mobility** | Fixed location (membrane/cytoplasm) | Diffuses freely through cytoplasm |
| **Specificity** | High (recognizes specific substrate) | Lower (many effectors responsive) |
| **Duration** | Longer (protein stability) | Transient (rapid degradation) |
| **Function** | Executes response | Relays/amplifies signal |
| **Example** | PKA phosphorylates glycogen synthase | cAMP activates PKA |
| **Regulation** | Phosphorylation/dephosphorylation | Synthesis/degradation rate |

**KEY PROPERTIES OF SECOND MESSENGERS**:

1. **SMALL SIZE** — Allow free diffusion through water-filled cytoplasm
2. **RAPID SYNTHESIS** — Enzymatic production (no need for new transcription)
3. **RAPID DEGRADATION** — Phosphodiesterase (cAMP), phosphatase (IP₃), re-uptake (Ca²⁺)
   • Ensures signal termination when stimulus removed
4. **HIGH AFFINITY FOR EFFECTORS** — Low Kd binding to downstream targets
5. **AMPLIFICATION POTENTIAL** — One source (e.g., adenylyl cyclase) → many messengers
6. **NON-GENETIC ENCODING** — Signal levels don't depend on transcription (fast response possible)
7. **REVERSIBILITY** — On-off switching through synthesis-degradation balance

💡 Key Principle: Second messengers bridge TEMPORAL GAP between fast receptor activation and slow gene expression responses.

📚 References: [Alberts Ch.15, "Second Messengers"] + [Lehninger Ch.12, "cAMP, IP₃, Ca²⁺ Systems"]

[Q010] Cell Communication Comprehensive (2018, 14 marks)

🎯 KEYWORD BREAKDOWN:
• "Relay proteins" = transducers = G-proteins, kinases, adapter proteins
• "Signal termination" = "off-switching" = how signals stop

📝 MNEMONIC:
🔡 "RAG-FLT" = Receptors Amplify Gating - Fast, Long-lasting, Transient termination

✍️ COMPREHENSIVE SOLUTION (700+ words across 3 parts):

a) **Cell-to-Cell Communication + Basic Components** (5 marks)

[See Q008 definition + new details on signal relay]:

**Basic Components Detailed Mechanism**:

1. **LIGAND** (সংকেত অণু) — Hormone/growth factor/neurotransmitter
   • Binds receptor in 1:1 stoichiometry (high specificity)
   • Carries concentration information (more ligand = stronger signal)

2. **RECEPTOR** (গ্রাহক) — Membrane or nuclear protein
   • Acts as "signal decoder" (recognizes only correct ligand)
   • Multiple copies per cell (100–1,000,000 depending on receptor type)
   • Regulated expression allows cell to adjust sensitivity

3. **INTRACELLULAR RELAY PROTEINS** (অন্তঃকোশীয় রিলে) — Signal amplifiers
   • G-proteins: Gα (exchanges GDP↔GTP), Gβγ dimer (also signals)
   • Kinases: Add phosphate groups to downstream targets
   • Adapter proteins: Grb2 (recruits SOS to membrane for Ras activation)
   • PLC: Cleaves PIP₂ → IP₃ + DAG

4. **SECOND MESSENGERS** (দ্বিতীয় বার্তা) — Mobile signal carriers
   • Amplify & spread signal through cell
   • cAMP (PKA activator), IP₃ (Ca²⁺ releaser), Ca²⁺, DAG (PKC activator)

5. **EFFECTOR PROTEINS** (প্রভাবক) — Response executers
   • Kinases (PKA, ERK, PKC) phosphorylate downstream targets
   • Phosphatases remove phosphates (negative regulation)
   • Channels open/close in response to Ca²⁺ or PKA

6. **RESPONSE MACHINERY** (প্রতিক্রিয়া কাঠামো) — End-stage responders
   • Glycogen phosphorylase (breaks down glycogen when phosphorylated by PKA)
   • CREB transcription factor (phosphorylated by PKA → enters nucleus → activates genes)
   • Ion channels (hyperpolarize/depolarize based on Ca²⁺ or cAMP)

**Signal Relay Cascade Visualization**:
```
Ligand (Epinephrine)
    ↓
Receptor (β-adrenergic) [DETECTION]
    ↓
G-protein (Gαs, Gβγ) [RELAY 1]
    ↓
Adenylyl Cyclase [RELAY 2]
    ↓
cAMP (Second Messenger) [AMPLIFICATION ×10-100]
    ↓
PKA (Effector Protein) [RESPONSE EXECUTOR]
    ↓
Phosphorylates Glycogen Phosphorylase [OUTCOME]
    ↓
Glycogen → Glucose [CELLULAR RESPONSE]
```

b) **Signal Transduction Definition + General Features** (5 marks)

**Signal Transduction** (সংকেত পরিবহন) Definition:
= Conversion of extracellular signal (ligand binding) into intracellular biochemical events
= Process: Recognition → Amplification → Integration → Response

**General Features** (সাধারণ বৈশিষ্ট্য):

1. **SPECIFICITY** (নির্দিষ্টতা):
   • Each receptor recognizes specific ligand(s) only
   • Prevents crosstalk between unrelated signals
   • Achieved through precise 3D binding pocket geometry

2. **AMPLIFICATION** (বর্ধন):
   • Cascade multiplication: receptor → 10 G-proteins → 100 cAMP
   • Allows cell to respond to nanomolar hormone concentrations
   • Each step multiplies signal strength

3. **INTEGRATION** (সমন্বয়):
   • Cell compares multiple signal inputs simultaneously
   • Transcription factors receive input from 2-10+ kinases
   • Final response = weighted sum of all inputs
   • Example: IL-2 production requires BOTH TCR + co-stimulatory signals

4. **FEEDBACK CONTROL** (প্রতিক্রিয়া নিয়ন্ত্রণ):
   • **Negative feedback**: output inhibits its own pathway
     - Example: High cAMP → PKA phosphorylates PDE (phosphodiesterase) → ↑ cAMP degradation → signal OFF
   • **Positive feedback**: output amplifies own pathway
     - Example: Ca²⁺ → CaMKII autophosphorylation → ↑ enzyme activity (memory mechanism)

5. **SIGNAL TERMINATION** (সংকেত বন্ধ):
   • Without termination, cell becomes insensitive to new signals
   • Mechanisms:
     - Receptor desensitization (β-arrestin binding, internalization)
     - Second messenger degradation (phosphodiesterase breaks cAMP, phosphatases remove phosphates)
     - Enzyme inactivation (protein synthesis stops without persistent stimulus)
     - Gene transcription turns OFF (no sustained transcription factor activation)

6. **ADAPTATION** (খাপ খাওয়ানো):
   • Prolonged signal → decreased response (desensitization)
   • Allows cell to detect CHANGES in signal, not absolute levels
   • Example: Olfactory neurons adapt to constant odor (can still detect new odors)
   • Mechanism: Feedback loops reduce effector sensitivity

**Key Features Table**:

| Feature | Mechanism | Example | Significance |
|---------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Specificity | Receptor-ligand binding geometry | β-AR binds epinephrine not dopamine | Targeted responses |
| Amplification | Cascade multiplication | 1 GPCR → 1000 cAMP | Detects low signals |
| Integration | Convergence on shared effectors | ERK phosphorylated by ERK1/2 kinases from RTK & GPCR | Context-dependent response |
| Feedback | Auto-regulation of pathway | cAMP inhibits adenylyl cyclase | Prevents positive runaway |
| Termination | Degradation & desensitization | PDE breaks cAMP; β-arrestin blocks GPCR | Prevents over-signaling |
| Adaptation | Sensitivity reduction over time | Olfactory neuron stops responding to constant odor | Dynamic range preserved |

c) **Effector Proteins & Second Messengers; Comparison** (4 marks)

[See Q009b detailed comparison table above]

Additional distinction:

**Spatial organization matters**:
• Effector proteins often membrane-associated or in specific compartments
  - PKA anchored near specific substrates via A-kinase anchoring proteins (AKAPs)
  - Results in localized, targeted phosphorylation
  
• Second messengers diffuse widely
  - cAMP spreads through entire cell
  - Ca²⁺ waves propagate through ER + cytoplasm
  - Can activate many effectors simultaneously

**Temporal dynamics**:
• Effectors: Longer acting (protein turnover = hours-days)
• Second messengers: Transient (seconds to minutes)
• Allows rapid on/off signal control despite slower protein synthesis

[Q011] Features of Different Signaling Types (2020, 5 marks)

🎯 KEYWORD BREAKDOWN:
• "Features" = characteristic properties distinguishing each type
• Speed, distance, specificity = 3 key differentiators

📝 MNEMONIC:
🔡 "SPE-Dist" = SPEed, Distance, specificity; Efficiency, temporal dynamics, integration speed

✍️ COMPREHENSIVE SOLUTION (5 marks):

**Features of Five Signaling Types** (পাঁচ সংকেত পদ্ধতির বৈশিষ্ট্য):

| Type | Distance | Speed | Specificity | Duration | Physiological Role | Speed Ranking |
|------|----------|-------|-------------|----------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Synaptic** | Nanometers (20-40 nm) | ⚡⚡⚡ FASTEST (1-10 ms) | Point-to-point (one synapse) | Brief (ms-sec) | Immediate motor/sensory | #1 |
| **Juxtacrine** | Contact-dependent (nm) | ⚡⚡⚡ FAST (10-100 ms) | Cell-cell recognition | Variable | Development, immunity | #2 |
| **Paracrine** | Local (micrometers) | ⚡⚡ MEDIUM (sec-min) | Neighborhood (100-200 μm spread) | Moderate (min) | Tissue repair, immunity | #3 |
| **Neuroendocrine** | Long (cm-meters) | ⚡ MEDIUM-SLOW (sec-min) | Broadcast to select organs | Long (min-hrs) | Stress response, circadian | #4 |
| **Endocrine** | Systemic (meters via blood) | 🐢 SLOW (min-hrs) | Broadcast (all cells express receptor) | Very long (hrs-days) | Metabolism, development, homeostasis | #5 |

**Detailed Feature Explanations**:

🔸**Speed Rationale**:
• Synaptic = direct vesicular release → immediate post-synaptic response (no diffusion delay)
• Paracrine = short diffusion distance → faster than systemic
• Endocrine = ligand crosses tissue → enters bloodstream → travels throughout body → reaches target

🔸**Specificity Rationale**:
• Synaptic = physical synaptic specialization (not all neurons contact all neurons)
• Paracrine = diffusion gradient limits range (recipients are neighbors)
• Endocrine = receptor density determines response (all cells can receive signal, but only responders have receptors)

🔸**Physiological Advantages**:
• Synaptic: Immediate muscle control, fast reflexes, sensory acuity
• Paracrine: Wound healing (growth factors from wounded area attract immune cells nearby)
• Endocrine: Coordinated body-wide responses (stress response: adrenal cortisol + medulla epinephrine simultaneously)

📚 Reference: [Alberts Ch.15, "Principles of Signaling Types"]

[Q012] Cell Signaling Types & Concepts (2022, 12 marks)

🎯 KEYWORD BREAKDOWN:
• "Mode of action" = step-by-step mechanism (how GPCR works)

📝 MNEMONIC:
🔡 "GPCR-LIG-GAP" = G-protein Coupled Protein Receptor - Ligand bindING - GAP junction alternative

✍️ COMPREHENSIVE SOLUTION (4 parts, 12 marks total):

a) **Define Cell Signaling + Different Types** (1+4 = 5 marks)

[See Q001 definition + Q005 types]

Definition: Cell signaling (কোষ সংকেত) = organized communication system enabling cells to sense environment & coordinate behaviors.

b) **Release & Transport Mechanism of Signal Molecules** (3 marks)

**Signal Release Mechanisms** (সংকেত নিঃসরণ):

1. **Exocytosis** (অনুকোশ স্থানান্তর):
   • Vesicles transport signal molecules (hormones, neurotransmitters) to plasma membrane
   • SNARE proteins mediate fusion with membrane
   • Contents released to extracellular space
   • Used by: neurons (neurotransmitters), pancreatic cells (insulin), immune cells (cytokines)

2. **Constitutive Release**:
   • Steady-state secretion (not stimulus-triggered)
   • Example: Continuous growth factor production by fibroblasts

3. **Regulated Exocytosis**:
   • Stimulus-triggered vesicle fusion
   • Example: Nerve action potential → Ca²⁺ influx → SNARE activation → ACh release

**Transport Mechanisms** (পরিবহন পদ্ধতি):

1. **Aqueous Transport** (জল-ভিত্তিক):
   • Hydrophilic ligands travel freely in blood
   • Insulin, epinephrine, peptide hormones
   • No carrier protein needed (but some use carriers for stability)

2. **Protein-Bound Transport** (প্রোটিন-বন্ধন):
   • Hydrophobic ligands require carrier proteins in blood
   • Cortisol: 95% bound to cortisol-binding globulin
   • Thyroid hormone: 99.9% bound to thyroid-binding globulin
   • Ensures stability + regulated availability (free fraction = active)

3. **Local Diffusion** (স্থানীয় বিস্তার):
   • Paracrine factors diffuse through interstitial space
   • Establish concentration gradients (high near source, low at distance)
   • Gradient determines pathway strength

4. **Vesicular Transport** (অনুকোশীয় বহন):
   • Secretory vesicles carry packed hormones/factors
   • Transport from ER → Golgi → plasma membrane
   • Ensures high local concentration upon release

**Life Cycle Table**:

| Stage | Process | Time | Regulation |
|-------|---------|------|-----------|
| **Synthesis** | Transcription + Translation | Hours-days | Gene expression control |
| **Packaging** | ER→Golgi→Vesicles | Minutes | Vesicular machinery |
| **Release** | Exocytosis (signal-dependent) | Seconds-minutes | Ca²⁺, SNARE proteins |
| **Transport** | Diffusion or blood carrier | Minutes-hours | Carrier affinity, blood flow |
| **Delivery** | Reach target cell | Variable | Distance & transport method |
| **Clearance** | Degradation + re-uptake | Minutes-hours | Phosphatases, receptors |

c) **Differences: Hydrophilic vs Hydrophobic Signaling Molecules** (2 marks)

[See Q006c + Q012 (below)]:

| Property | HYDROPHILIC | HYDROPHOBIC |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Solubility** | Water-soluble | Fat-soluble |
| **Transport in Blood** | Free (or protein-bound for stability) | Carrier proteins mandatory |

d) **Illustrate Mode of Action of GPCR** (2 marks)

**GPCR Mode of Action** (GPCR কর্মপ্রণালী):

[7-transmembrane G-protein coupled receptor mechanism]:

```
STEP 1: LIGAND BINDING
─────────────────────
Extracellular side: Ligand (epinephrine) approaches receptor
Binding pocket: Between transmembrane helices (5 & 6)
Result: Ligand-induced conformational change

STEP 2: RECEPTOR ACTIVATION
──────────────────────────
Conformational change exposes INTRACELLULAR domains
Cytoplasmic loops now presented as binding surface
Signal competent receptor ready for G-protein interaction

STEP 3: G-PROTEIN ACTIVATION
──────────────────────────
G-protein (α, β, γ subunits) docked at receptor
Receptor acts as GEF = Guanine nucleotide Exchange Factor
Gα subunit: GDP leaves → GTP enters
Gα-GTP dissociates from Gβγ

STEP 4: EFFECTOR ACTIVATION
──────────────────────────
Gα-GTP → Activates adenylyl cyclase (Gs) OR inhibits (Gi) depending on receptor subtype
Gβγ → Can also activate effectors (PLC, ion channels)
Second messenger formation: ATP → cAMP (via adenylyl cyclase)

STEP 5: DOWNSTREAM CASCADE
─────────────────────────
cAMP → Binds PKA regulatory subunit
PKA regulatory subunit releases catalytic subunit
Active PKA → Phosphorylates target proteins (glycogen phosphorylase, CREB, etc.)

STEP 6: SIGNAL TERMINATION
─────────────────────────
GTP hydrolysis: Gα has intrinsic GTPase activity (aided by RGS proteins = Regulators of G-protein Signaling)
Gα-GDP → Reassociates with Gβγ (cycling back to inactive state)
cAMP degradation: Phosphodiesterase (PDE) breaks cAMP → AMP
PKA loses substrate activation (cAMP binding removed)

RESULT: Signal OFF, ready for next stimulus
```

**STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS DIAGRAM**:

```
    EXTRACELLULAR
    ─────────────
    Epinephrine ←─ ligand
           ↓
      [GPCR binding pocket]
           ↓
    CONFORMATIONAL CHANGE
    
    INTRACELLULAR
    ──────────────
    G-protein (released)
           ↓
    Adenylyl cyclase ← activation
           ↓
    cAMP ← second messenger (↑↑ amplification)
           ↓
    PKA ← activation (binds cAMP)
           ↓
    Phosphorylates glycogen phosphorylase kinase
           ↓
    Glycogen phosphorylase ← active
           ↓
    Glycogen → Glucose-1-PO₄ (glucose release)
           ↓
    CELLULAR RESPONSE: Energy mobilization ✅
    
    TERMINATION (negative feedback):
    ← GTPase activity (Gα-GTP → Gα-GDP)
    ← Phosphodiesterase (cAMP → AMP)
    ← Receptor desensitization (β-arrestin binding)
```

**KEY REGULATORY POINTS** (നിയന്ത്രണ പോയിന്റുകൾ):

1. **GDP-GTP Exchange** (not enzyme-catalyzed):
   • Receptor acts as GEF (not an enzyme, just facilitates exchange)
   • Why? Receptor distorts G-protein shape → GDP pops out → GTP enters from cytoplasm (billions of GTP > GDP)

2. **Signal Duration Controlled by GTP Hydrolysis**:
   • Gα has slow GTPase activity (intrinsic stability)
   • RGS proteins accelerate GTPase ("off-switch accelerators")
   • Different proteins = different signal duration (flexibility in signaling)

3. **Amplification at Each Level**:
   • 1 GPCR → 10 G-proteins → 100 adenylyl cyclase → 1000 cAMP → 10,000 PKA → 100,000 phosphorylated substrates
   • Result: Nanomolar hormone → cellular response
